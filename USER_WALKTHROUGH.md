## ✈️ Flight Logbook

Click "**Logbook**" in the left sidebar.

### Viewing Your Flights
- See all your flight entries in a table
- Each entry shows:
  - Date of flight
  - Aircraft registration
  - Aircraft type
  - Flight time (hours)
  - Route (departure → destination)
  - Pilot role (PIC, Dual, Solo)
  - Flight conditions (Day/Night, VFR/IFR)

### Adding a New Flight
1. Click the **"+ Add Flight Entry"** button (blue button at top)
2. Fill out the form:
   - **Date**: When you flew
   - **Aircraft**: Registration number (e.g., VT-ABC)
   - **Type**: Aircraft model (e.g., Cessna 172)
   - **From/To**: Departure and destination airports
   - **Duration**: Flight time in hours
   - **Type of Time**: Select PIC, Dual, Solo, etc.
   - **Conditions**: Day/Night, VFR/IFR
   - **Landings**: Number of day/night landings
   - **Remarks**: Any notes about the flight
3. Click **"Save"**
4. Your flight appears in the list immediately!

### Editing/Deleting Flights
- Each entry has **Edit** and **Delete** buttons
- Click Edit to modify details
- Click Delete to remove (with confirmation)

## 🏥 Medical Certificates

Click "**Medicals**" in the left sidebar.

### Viewing Your Medical
You'll see your medical certificate card showing:
- **Class Type**: Class 1, 2, or 3
- **Status**: Valid / Expiring Soon / Expired (color-coded)
- **Issue Date**: When it was issued
- **Expiry Date**: When it expires
- **Aviation Medical Examiner (AME)**: Doctor's name and AME number
- **Examination Date**: When you had your medical exam
- **Restrictions**: Any medical restrictions (shown in red if present)
- **Limitations**: Operational limitations (if any)
- **Medical History**: Previous medical certificates

### Uploading/Renewing Your Medical
1. Click **"Renew Medical"** button (blue)
2. Fill out the form:
   - **Issue Date & Expiry Date**: From your medical certificate
   - **AME Name**: Your doctor's name (e.g., Dr. Jane Smith)
   - **AME Certificate Number**: The AME number
   - **Examination Date**: When you had the exam
   - **Restrictions**: Any restrictions (e.g., "Must wear corrective lenses")
   - **Limitations**: Any limitations (e.g., "Not valid for night flying")
   - **Remarks**: Additional notes
   - **Upload PDF**: Your medical certificate scan
3. Click **"Save"**
4. Your medical is now stored and displayed!

**💡 Tip**: The system will show warnings when your medical is expiring soon.

## 📜 Pilot Licenses

Click "**License**" in the left sidebar, then select your license type (e.g., "PPL").

### Viewing Your License
You'll see your license card showing:
- **License Number**
- **Issue & Expiry Dates**
- **Status**: Valid/Expiring/Expired
- **Restrictions**: Any license restrictions (shown in red warning box)
- **Ratings**: All your pilot ratings (shown as blue chips)
- **Endorsements**: Detailed cards for each endorsement

### Your Ratings
Ratings appear as blue badges with checkmarks:
- Instrument Rating ✓
- Multi-Engine ✓
- Seaplane ✓
- etc.

### Your Endorsements
Each endorsement shows:
- **Type**: High Performance, Complex, Tailwheel, etc.
- **Instructor**: Name and certificate number
- **Date**: When you received it
- **Aircraft**: Aircraft type used
- **Remarks**: Additional notes
- **Remove button** (X): To delete if needed

### Adding a Rating
1. Click **"+ Add Rating"** (purple button)
2. Select rating from dropdown:
   - Instrument
   - Multi-Engine
   - Seaplane
   - Glider
   - Helicopter
   - Type Rating
3. Click **"Save Rating"**
4. Rating appears immediately!

### Adding an Endorsement
1. Click **"+ Add Endorsement"** (green button)
2. Fill out the form:
   - **Type**: Select from dropdown (High Performance, Complex, Tailwheel, etc.)
   - **Instructor Name**: Your CFI's name
   - **Instructor Certificate**: CFI number
   - **Date**: When you received it
   - **Aircraft Type**: What aircraft was used
   - **Remarks**: Any notes
3. Click **"Save Endorsement"**
4. Endorsement card appears immediately!

### Removing an Endorsement
- Click the **X** button on any endorsement card
- Confirm deletion
- It's removed instantly

### Updating Your License
1. Click **"Renew/Update License"** (blue button)
2. Update issue/expiry dates
3. Upload new PDF if available
4. Click **"Save"**

## 🤖 AI Chatbot (Your Aviation Assistant)

Click "**Chatbot**" in the left sidebar.

### What Can You Ask?

The chatbot knows **your real flight data** and can answer:

#### About Your Flight Hours
- "How many flight hours do I have?"
- "What's my total PIC time?"
- "How many night hours have I logged?"

**Example Response**: "You have logged 10.3 total flight hours. Here's the breakdown: 5.2 hours PIC, 4.1 hours dual instruction, 1.0 hours solo, 2.3 hours night time..."

#### About Currency
- "Am I current to carry passengers?"
- "Am I night current?"
- "Can I fly passengers at night?"

**Example Response**: "Yes, you are current for day passenger operations. You completed 5 takeoffs and landings in the last 90 days. However, you are NOT current for night passengers..."

#### About Your Medical
- "When does my medical expire?"
- "Is my medical valid?"
- "Do I have any restrictions?"

**Example Response**: "Your Class 2 medical certificate expires on June 15, 2025 (in 145 days). It was issued by Dr. Jane Smith (AME #12345). You have the restriction: Must wear corrective lenses."

#### About Your License
- "What ratings do I have?"
- "What endorsements do I have?"
- "When does my license expire?"

**Example Response**: "You hold a Private Pilot License (PPL #12345) with the following ratings: Instrument Rating, Multi-Engine. You have endorsements for: High Performance, Complex Aircraft..."

#### General Aviation Questions
- "What are the requirements for a private pilot license?"
- "How do I stay current?"
- "What is IFR currency?"
- "Explain Class B airspace"

### Using the Chatbot
1. Type your question in the text box at the bottom
2. Click **"Send"** or press Enter
3. Watch as the chatbot:
   - Reads your actual data
   - Analyzes the question
   - Provides a personalized response
4. Ask follow-up questions!

**💡 Tip**: The chatbot has access to FAA regulations and your personal flight data, making responses both accurate and personalized!

## 💡 Quick Tips

### Best Practices
1. **Log flights immediately** after landing while details are fresh
2. **Upload documents** as soon as you receive them
3. **Check dashboard regularly** for expiring items
4. **Use the chatbot** to quickly check currency before flights
5. **Keep endorsements updated** as you complete training

### Common Tasks

**Before a Flight:**
- Check currency status via chatbot
- Verify medical is valid
- Review recent flight hours

**After a Flight:**
- Add logbook entry immediately
- Note any unusual conditions in remarks
- Update night/instrument time

**Monthly:**
- Review logbook for accuracy
- Check for expiring documents
- Update any endorsements or ratings

**Before Checkrides:**
- Print/export logbook summary
- Verify all endorsements are logged
- Check minimum hour requirements
